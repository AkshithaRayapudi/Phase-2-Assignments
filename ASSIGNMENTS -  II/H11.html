Lenovo is a multinational technology company headquartered in Beijing, China, and Morrisville, North Carolina, USA. Founded in 1984 as Legend, it later rebranded as Lenovo in 2004. Initially known for its personal computers (PCs), Lenovo has since expanded its product lineup to include smartphones, tablets, servers, workstations, and other electronic devices. As one of the world's largest PC vendors, Lenovo has a significant global presence. It offers a wide range of laptops, desktops, and accessories under various product lines such as the ThinkPad, IdeaPad, and Legion series. Lenovo's ThinkPad line, acquired from IBM in 2005, is particularly renowned for its durability, performance, and enterprise-grade features, making it popular among business professionals and IT departments. In addition to PCs, Lenovo also manufactures smartphones and tablets. Rolex is a Swiss luxury watch manufacturer founded in 1905 by Hans Wilsdorf and Alfred Davis. It is one of the most renowned and prestigious watch brands in the world, known for its quality, craftsmanship, and iconic designs. Rolex watches are often seen as status symbols and are highly sought after by collectors and enthusiasts. Rolex produces a wide range of wristwatches catering to various purposes, including diving, aviation, sports, and formal occasions. Some of its most famous watch models include the Rolex Submariner, Rolex Daytona, Rolex Datejust, Rolex GMT-Master II, and Rolex Day-Date. These watches are known for their durability, accuracy, and timeless aesthetics. Rolex is also recognized for its innovations in watchmaking, such as the development of the first waterproof wristwatch, the Oyster, in 1926, and the introduction of the first wristwatch with an automatically changing date, the Datejust, in 1945. Due to their high demand and limited production, Rolex watches often retain their value well over time and can even appreciate in price in the secondary market.
